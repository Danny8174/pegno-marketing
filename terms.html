4. The escrow service

Pegno holds client funds in safeguarded accounts on behalf of both parties to a deal. Funds are released to the provider only when milestones are approved by the client. Pegno acts as an escrow agent and payment service provider, not as a party to the underlying service contract between client and provider.

5. Fees

Pegno charges a 2.5% platform fee on the value of each completed transaction. This fee is deducted at the time of payment release. All fees are displayed clearly before you fund a deal. Fees are non-refundable once a milestone has been released.

  • Creating false or misleading deal descriptions
  • Harassing or threatening other users

7. Disputes

Pegno operates a three-tier dispute resolution process. Disputes should first be raised between the parties directly. If unresolved, either party may escalate to Pegno for review. Pegno's determination will be made within 15 business days. If dissatisfied with Pegno's determination, users may escalate to the relevant financial ombudsman.

8. Safeguarding

All client funds held in escrow are safeguarded in designated accounts at authorised credit institutions, separate from Pegno's own operational funds, in accordance with Regulation 23 of the Payment Services Regulations 2017.
